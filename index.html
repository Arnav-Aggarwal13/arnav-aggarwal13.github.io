about me

I’m an undergraduate at MIT studying Computer Science (AI & Decision Making) with a minor in Brain and Cognitive Sciences. I like building machine learning models that tell us something about how the brain works, and then turning those ideas into practical systems. Recently, this has meant reconstructing EEG from fMRI in sleep, designing human-aligned speech models, and building tools like ErgOCR to solve real-world problems for my rowing team.

research

Reconstructing EEG from fMRI
Lewis Lab · MIT · 2025–present
  • Developing neural network models that reconstruct EEG rhythms and spectrograms from simultaneously recorded fMRI, linking high-spatial and high-temporal resolution signals.
  • Showed that pretraining across subjects followed by per-subject finetuning improves predictions most for EEG features known to vary strongly between individuals.
Human-aligned speech perception models
Lab for Computational Audition (McDermott Lab) · MIT · 2025
  • Designed experiments comparing categorical perception in humans with a biologically-inspired speech recognition model.
  • Generated computational speech stimuli, ran human psychophysics-style experiments, and analyzed alignment between model behavior and human data.

ErgOCR – rowing workout transcription
Personal project · 2025
  • Built an end-to-end system to detect and transcribe rowing machine scores from photos of the monitor, using object detection, perspective correction, and OCR.
  • Achieved ~96% transcription accuracy and integrated the tool into MIT rowing’s score recording workflow, reducing manual data entry time by an order of magnitude.

Personal project · 2024
